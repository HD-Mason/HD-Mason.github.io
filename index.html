Ethics Statement

Regulations and Code of Conducts

This research is conducted in a manner aligned with the UK General Data Protection Act (GDPR) and the Research Ethics principles of both the University of Gloucestershire and New College Swindon.

Consent

When interviewing an individual recording and transcription will be made, at the time of the interview consent to record and transcribe the conversation will happen. For questionnaires, the consent will be obtained through implied consent. By submitting the survey, the candidate is indicating that they are happy with their data being manipulated and analysed for purposes of the dissertation.

Data Collected

All data which is collected will remain on New College Swindon and University of Gloucestershire systems (Office 365, Turnitin, Moodle).

Rights of Data Subjects

In line with the UK GDPR, all participants of the study have the following rights:

  • Right of access
  • Right of erasure
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to edit the data

Participant Confidentiality

The data which has been collected shall be collated and analysed. Specific participant names shall not be explicitly called out within the dissertation unless consent has been obtained.
